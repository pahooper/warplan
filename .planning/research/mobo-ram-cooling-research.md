# DDR5 MARKET SITUATION

## CRITICAL: DDR5 Pricing Crisis (March 2026)

The DDR5 market is in a severe pricing crisis driven by AI demand consuming manufacturing capacity.

### Key Facts:
- **Price increases**: DDR5 prices have risen 100-400% compared to late 2024 levels
- **Root cause**: Major memory makers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) shifted production toward HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI data centers, creating consumer DRAM shortage
- **32GB DDR5-6000 kits**: Currently $370-$500 (were $80-$120 in late 2024)
- **64GB DDR5-6000 kits**: Currently $500-$710+ (a 64GB kit now costs more than a PS5)
- **Outlook**: Prices expected to remain high through H1 2026; partial easing possible late 2026; stabilization not expected until 2027-2028
- **NAND flash also affected**: SSD prices have risen significantly too

### Strategy Recommendation:
Buy 32GB now at the best deal you can find. 64GB is overkill for pure gaming and not worth the current premium. Watch for Newegg promo codes -- they're one of the few ways to get decent deals.

## ATX 3.0 / 12VHPWR / 12V-2x6 Notes

**Key considerations for new GPUs:**
- All PSUs listed above include **ATX 3.1 compliance** with native **12V-2x6 connectors** (the successor to 12VHPWR)
- RTX 50-series GPUs use the 12V-2x6 connector
- **Do NOT use adapter cables** from old PSUs -- buy an ATX 3.1 unit with native cables
- ATX 3.1 PSUs handle GPU transient power spikes (up to 2x rated power for microseconds)
- For RTX 5090 (575W TDP): minimum 1000W PSU recommended
- For RTX 5080 (360W TDP): 850W is adequate
- For RTX 5070 Ti (300W TDP): 850W is comfortable
