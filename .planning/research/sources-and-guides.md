## Key Findings Summary

### CPU Consensus
- **9800X3D is the undisputed gaming king** — 30% faster than 14900K in gaming
- **9850X3D** (Jan 2026) is marginally faster, ~$50 more
- At 4K with a powerful GPU, the CPU gap narrows significantly (GPU-bottlenecked)
- $480 MSRP for 9800X3D, ~$530 for 9850X3D

### GPU Consensus
- **RTX 5090** ($2,000 MSRP, $2,200+ street): Best 4K card, 20-50% over 4090, 30-69% over 5080. 32GB GDDR7. 575W TDP.
- **RTX 5080** ($999 MSRP, $1,150-1,250 street): "Boring" generationally (7-20% over 4080S). But still excellent for 4K gaming. 16GB GDDR7. 360W TDP.
- **RTX 5070 Ti** ($750 MSRP, $850-1,000 street): ~60 FPS 4K native, strong with DLSS. GN says "Do Not Buy" at inflated prices.
- **RTX 4080 Super**: Discontinued/scarce, was $999. Still a solid 4K card if found at good price.
- **RTX 5080 Super**: Delayed indefinitely, don't wait for it.

### RAM Crisis
- DDR5 32GB kits: $180-360+ (were $100-130)
- DDR5 64GB kits: $720+
- AI datacenter demand consuming 20% of DRAM production
- Relief not expected until late 2027-2028
- DDR5-6000 CL30 is optimal for AMD Ryzen gaming
- Pre-builts can be better value than DIY right now due to OEM RAM pricing
